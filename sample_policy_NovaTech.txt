POLICY 1: REMOTE WORK POLICY
Policy ID: POL-RW-NOVA-2025
================================================================================

Section 1.0 — Purpose
This policy establishes the guidelines and requirements for remote work arrangements
at NovaTech Solutions Inc. It aims to maintain productivity, security, and compliance
while providing flexibility to eligible employees.

Section 2.0 — Scope and Eligibility
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of NovaTech Solutions Inc.
Contractors and temporary employees are NOT covered by this policy and are ineligible
for remote work arrangements under these terms.

Employees must complete a 90-day probationary period before becoming eligible for
remote work. Employees who joined less than 90 days ago are not eligible regardless
of role or manager approval.

Section 3.0 — Approval Process
Remote work requests must be submitted in writing to the employee's direct manager
at least 5 business days in advance. Approval requires sign-off from both the direct
manager and the HR department. Verbal approvals are not valid.

Section 4.0 — Duration and International Work

Section 4.1 — Domestic Remote Work
Employees may work remotely within their home country for up to 180 days per calendar
year with standard manager approval.

Section 4.2 — International Remote Work
Working remotely from a foreign country for up to 30 days requires advance written
notification to HR at least 2 weeks prior. Stays of 31 or more days require legal
and tax review plus written approval from both the VP of HR and the Legal department.
Unauthorized international remote work beyond the approved period will result in the
employee bearing full personal liability for any resulting tax obligations.

Section 4.3 — Benefits Gap
NovaTech's health insurance plan does not provide coverage outside the employee's
home country. Employees working internationally must obtain independent travel health
insurance at their own expense before departure.

Section 5.0 — Equipment and Security

Section 5.1 — Company Equipment
Employees must use company-issued laptops for all remote work. Use of personal devices
for work requiring access to company systems is prohibited unless covered by the BYOD
policy (POL-IS-NOVA-2025 Section 6.0).

Section 5.2 — VPN Requirement
All remote employees must connect to the company VPN at all times when accessing
internal systems, databases, or client data. Working without VPN connection is a
security violation subject to disciplinary action.

Section 5.3 — Local Storage Prohibition
Storing company data, client information, or proprietary materials on personal or
local device storage is strictly prohibited. All data must be stored in approved
cloud systems (OneDrive, SharePoint). This prohibition applies even if the data is
encrypted — encryption does not exempt an employee from this requirement.

POLICY 2: DATA PRIVACY POLICY
Policy ID: POL-DP-NOVA-2025
================================================================================

Section 1.0 — Purpose
This policy governs the collection, use, storage, and transfer of personal data by
NovaTech Solutions Inc. in compliance with applicable privacy regulations.

Section 2.0 — Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and vendors who handle personal
data on behalf of NovaTech Solutions Inc.

Section 3.0 — Data Classification

Section 3.1 — Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
PII includes names, email addresses, phone numbers, physical addresses, Social
Security Numbers (SSNs), financial account numbers, and any data that can identify
an individual. SSNs and financial account numbers are classified as Sensitive PII
and require the highest level of protection.

Section 3.2 — Prohibited Data Handling
Sensitive PII (including SSNs) must never be transmitted via unencrypted email,
personal messaging apps, or any channel not approved by the Information Security team.
Verbal manager approval does not authorize transmission of Sensitive PII through
unapproved channels.

Section 4.0 — Data Transfers

Section 4.1 — Domestic Transfers
Data transfers within the country are permitted through approved secure channels
with appropriate access controls.

Section 4.2 — International Transfers (EEA)
Any transfer of personal data to countries in the European Economic Area (EEA)
requires a valid legal transfer mechanism such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
or Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) approved by the Data Protection Officer (DPO).
Transfers without the required safeguards are a violation of GDPR and this policy.

Section 5.0 — Data Protection Officer

Section 5.1 — DPO Role
NovaTech's Data Protection Officer (DPO) is responsible for overseeing all data
protection activities. The DPO must be consulted for any new data processing activity
involving customer PII before that activity commences.

Section 5.4 — DPO Consultation Requirement
Any new customer PII data flow — including new integrations, vendor relationships,
or product features involving PII — requires written DPO consultation and sign-off
before implementation.

Section 6.0 — Data Retention
Personal data must not be retained beyond the purpose for which it was collected.
Default retention periods: customer transaction data (7 years), employee records
(5 years after termination), marketing data (2 years). Data must be securely
deleted after the retention period using approved deletion methods.

POLICY 3: INFORMATION SECURITY POLICY
Policy ID: POL-IS-NOVA-2025
================================================================================

Section 1.0 — Purpose
This policy establishes the information security requirements for all employees and
systems at NovaTech Solutions Inc. to protect company data, client information, and
intellectual property.

Section 2.0 — Scope
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties with access to
NovaTech systems, networks, or data.

Section 3.0 — Access Control

Section 3.1 — Least Privilege
Employees must be granted the minimum level of system access necessary to perform
their job functions. Requests for elevated access require manager and IT Security
approval with documented business justification.

Section 3.2 — Password Requirements
All passwords must be at least 14 characters, include uppercase, lowercase, numbers,
and special characters, and must not be reused within the last 12 password cycles.
Passwords must be changed every 90 days.

Section 4.0 — Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Section 4.1 — MFA Requirement
MFA is mandatory for all employee accounts accessing company systems, including
email, VPN, cloud storage, and internal applications. Employees may not request
exemptions from MFA regardless of role.

Section 5.0 — Encryption and Storage

Section 5.1 — Encryption at Rest
All company laptops must have full-disk encryption enabled (BitLocker for Windows,
FileVault for macOS). IT Security will verify encryption status during onboarding
and quarterly audits.

Section 5.3 — Local Storage Ban
Storing sensitive company or client data on local device storage is strictly
prohibited even if encrypted. All sensitive data must reside in approved cloud
storage systems. This applies to all devices including company laptops, personal
laptops, USB drives, and external hard drives.

Section 6.0 — BYOD Policy
Personal devices may access company email and calendar only through the approved
Mobile Device Management (MDM) solution. Personal devices must be enrolled in MDM
and comply with the same encryption and password requirements as company devices.
Personal devices may not be used to access client data, financial systems, or any
system classified as Confidential or Restricted.

Section 7.0 — Incident Reporting
Any suspected security incident, data breach, unauthorized access, or loss of
company device must be reported to IT Security within 2 hours of discovery. Delayed
reporting is itself a policy violation subject to disciplinary action.

Section 8.0 — Security Training
All employees must complete mandatory security awareness training within 30 days of
joining and annually thereafter. Employees who do not complete training within the
required window will have their system access suspended until training is complete.

POLICY 4: CODE OF CONDUCT POLICY
Policy ID: POL-COC-NOVA-2025
================================================================================

Section 1.0 — Purpose
This Code of Conduct establishes the standards of professional behaviour expected
of all NovaTech Solutions employees and defines the consequences for violations.

Section 2.0 — Scope
This policy applies to all full-time employees, part-time employees, contractors,
and vendors representing NovaTech Solutions Inc. in any capacity.

Section 3.0 — Harassment and Discrimination

Section 3.1 — Zero Tolerance
NovaTech maintains a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, discrimination, or
retaliation of any kind. This includes sexual harassment, racial discrimination,
bullying, intimidation, and any unwelcome conduct based on a protected characteristic.

Section 3.2 — Reporting Harassment
Employees who experience or witness harassment must report it to their HR Business
Partner or directly to the VP of Human Resources. Reports may also be submitted
anonymously via the Ethics Hotline at 1-800-555-0199. Retaliation against anyone
who reports harassment in good faith is strictly prohibited and is itself a
terminable offence.

Section 4.0 — Conflicts of Interest
Employees must disclose any personal, financial, or professional relationship that
could create a conflict of interest with NovaTech's business activities. Disclosures
must be submitted in writing to the employee's manager and the Legal department
within 30 days of the conflict arising.

Section 5.0 — Confidentiality
Employees must protect NovaTech's confidential information including trade secrets,
client lists, financial data, product roadmaps, and proprietary technology. This
obligation continues for 2 years after the employee's last day of employment.
Sharing confidential information with competitors or unauthorized third parties is
grounds for immediate termination and legal action.

Section 6.0 — Disciplinary Procedures

Section 6.1 — Progressive Discipline
Minor violations are subject to progressive discipline: verbal warning, written
warning, final written warning, and termination. The severity and frequency of
violations determine which stage of discipline applies.

Section 6.2 — Summary Dismissal
Serious violations including theft, fraud, physical violence, harassment, deliberate
data breach, or sharing confidential information with competitors may result in
immediate termination without the progressive discipline process.

Section 7.0 — Anti-Retaliation
Any employee found to have retaliated against a colleague for making a good-faith
report of a policy violation will face immediate disciplinary action up to and
including termination, regardless of whether the original complaint was sustained.
