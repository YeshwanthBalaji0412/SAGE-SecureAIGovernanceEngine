Section 1: Purpose and Scope
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Purpose
This handbook establishes the academic standards, conduct expectations, and
institutional policies for Crestwood University ("the University").

1.2 Scope
Part A (Sections 2–4): Applies to all enrolled undergraduate and graduate students.
Part B (Sections 5–6): Applies to all full-time and adjunct faculty.
Administrative staff are governed by the separate Staff Employment Policy.
Visiting scholars and exchange students are subject to Section 3 (Academic Integrity)
and Section 4 (Campus Conduct) only.

1.3 Acknowledgement
Students must acknowledge this handbook during registration each academic year.
Faculty must acknowledge at the start of each appointment or renewal.


Section 2: Academic Records and Privacy
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Student Records
The University protects student education records in accordance with applicable
privacy principles. Education records include: grades, transcripts, enrollment
status, financial aid records, and disciplinary records.

2.2 Directory Information
The following may be disclosed without student consent: full name, enrollment
status, degree program, graduation date, and honors received.
Students may opt out of directory information disclosure by submitting a written
request to the Registrar's Office by the end of the first week of each semester.

2.3 Non-Directory Information
The following require written student consent before disclosure: GPA, grades,
Social Security numbers, financial records, health records, and disciplinary files.
Exception: Emergency disclosure is permitted when there is an imminent threat to
health or safety. Such disclosures must be documented within 24 hours.

2.4 Parental Access
Parents of financially dependent students under age 24 may request academic
records with proof of dependency. After age 24 or financial independence
(whichever comes first), only the student controls record access.

2.5 Data Breach
Any breach of student records must be reported to the University Privacy Officer
within 4 hours of discovery. Affected students must be notified within 30 days.

Section 3: Academic Integrity
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Core Principle
All work submitted for academic credit must represent the student's own original
effort unless the instructor explicitly authorizes collaboration in writing.

3.2 Prohibited Conduct
  a) Plagiarism: submitting another person's words, ideas, or code as one's own
     without proper citation, regardless of the original source.
  b) Fabrication: inventing or falsifying data, citations, experimental results,
     or any portion of an academic submission.
  c) Unauthorized Collaboration: working with others on individual assessments
     unless the instructor has explicitly authorized it in the course syllabus.
  d) AI-Generated Submissions: using AI tools (e.g., ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot)
     to generate text, code, or analysis submitted for credit without explicit
     written instructor authorization. Submitting AI-generated work without
     authorization is treated as plagiarism.
  e) Contract Cheating: purchasing, commissioning, or downloading completed
     assignments from any third-party service.
  f) Exam Misconduct: using unauthorized materials, devices, or assistance during
     any examination, quiz, or timed assessment.

3.3 Consequences
  First offense: Zero on the assignment; Academic Integrity Warning on record.
  Second offense: Failing grade (F) for the entire course.
  Third offense: Academic suspension for one full semester; notation on transcript.
  Egregious cases (contract cheating, impersonation, repeated fabrication):
     Expulsion from the University with permanent transcript notation.
  Consequences apply cumulatively across all courses and semesters.

3.4 Reporting
Faculty must report suspected violations to the Dean of Academic Affairs within
5 business days of discovery. Students may appeal within 14 calendar days of
receiving written notice of the outcome.

3.5 Appeals
The Academic Integrity Board reviews appeals and must issue a decision within
30 calendar days. Board decisions are final.


Section 4: Campus Conduct and Safety
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Non-Discrimination
Crestwood University prohibits discrimination and harassment based on race,
color, national origin, gender, gender identity, sexual orientation, age,
disability, religion, or any other protected characteristic.
Reports must be directed to the Equal Opportunity Office within 60 days of
the incident.
Equal Opportunity Office: equity@crestwooduniv.edu | +1 (617) 555-0144

4.2 Prohibited Items
The following are prohibited on all University property and at University events:
  - Firearms, knives with blades over 3 inches, and all other weapons
  - Illegal substances
  - Alcohol (except at licensed, University-approved events for those 21+)

4.3 Alcohol Policy
Students under 21 may not possess or consume alcohol on campus under any
circumstances, including private dormitory rooms.
Students 21 and over may consume alcohol in private residential spaces only;
open containers are not permitted in any common or public campus area.

4.4 Smoking and Vaping
Smoking and vaping are prohibited inside all University buildings, within 25 feet
of any building entrance, and in all open-air athletic facilities.
Designated smoking areas are marked on the campus map.

4.5 Noise and Quiet Hours
Residential quiet hours: 11:00 PM – 8:00 AM Sunday through Thursday;
1:00 AM – 10:00 AM Friday and Saturday.
Violations may result in a formal conduct warning or residential probation.


Section 5: Faculty Responsibilities
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Syllabus Requirements
All faculty must publish a course syllabus on the University LMS by the first day
of class. The syllabus must include: learning objectives, grading breakdown,
attendance policy, academic integrity statement, and AI tool use policy.

5.2 Grade Submission Deadlines
Final grades must be submitted in the University system within 5 business days
of the last exam or final assignment. Late grade submission may delay student
graduation and is a violation of this policy.

5.3 Availability
All full-time faculty must hold a minimum of 3 office hours per week.
Adjunct faculty must hold a minimum of 1 office hour per week per course taught.
Office hours may be offered in person or via video conference.

5.4 Conflict of Interest
Faculty must disclose any romantic or close personal relationship with a current
student in their course to the Department Chair before the semester begins.
The student must be reassigned to another instructor's section where possible.

5.5 Research Misconduct
Fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism in research activities must be reported
to the Office of Research Integrity within 10 business days of discovery.
Research Integrity: integrity@crestwooduniv.edu | +1 (617) 555-0188


Section 6: Faculty Leave and Absence
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6.1 Sabbatical Leave
Tenured faculty are eligible for sabbatical leave after 6 consecutive years of
full-time service. Sabbatical is granted for 1 full academic year at 70% salary,
or 1 semester at full salary. Applications must be submitted to the Provost's
Office by March 1 for the following academic year.

6.2 Medical Leave
Faculty are entitled to up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected medical leave per
academic year for documented serious health conditions. Salary continuation
(up to 8 weeks) is provided for conditions requiring hospitalization.

6.3 Parental Leave
Primary caregiver faculty receive 1 semester of paid leave at full salary.
Secondary caregiver faculty receive 6 weeks of paid leave at full salary.

6.4 Class Coverage Responsibility
Faculty on approved leave must arrange class coverage with the Department Chair
at least 3 weeks before the leave begins. Failure to arrange coverage may result
in salary deduction for uncovered class sessions.


Section 7: IT and Technology Use
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7.1 Authorized Use
University technology resources (networks, systems, software, email) are provided
for academic and administrative purposes. Limited personal use is permitted provided
it does not interfere with work duties or consume excessive bandwidth.

7.2 Prohibited Activities
  - Accessing or distributing illegal content of any kind
  - Cryptocurrency mining on University infrastructure
  - Sharing login credentials; each account is strictly individual
  - Installing unauthorized software on University-owned devices
  - Attempting to bypass network security or monitoring controls

7.3 Privacy Expectation
Users have no expectation of privacy on University-owned systems, networks, or
devices. The University may monitor traffic, logs, and communications on its
infrastructure without prior notice.

7.4 Devices on Campus Wi-Fi
Personal devices connected to the University's Wi-Fi network (including student
laptops, phones, and tablets) are subject to network traffic monitoring for
security purposes.

7.5 Data Storage
University research data and student records must never be stored on personal
cloud services (Google Drive personal accounts, Dropbox personal, iCloud).
Use only University-provided OneDrive or approved institutional repositories.
