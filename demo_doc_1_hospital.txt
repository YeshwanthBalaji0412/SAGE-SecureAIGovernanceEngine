Section 1: Purpose and Scope
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

1.1 Purpose
This manual governs the conduct, responsibilities, and operational standards
for all personnel employed at Riverside General Hospital ("the Hospital").

1.2 Scope
Applies to all full-time and part-time employees including clinical staff,
administrative staff, and support staff.
Visiting physicians and medical students on rotation are subject to the Code
of Conduct (Section 3) and Patient Privacy (Section 4) only.
Independent contractors and agency nurses are NOT covered by this manual;
refer to their individual contracts.

1.3 Effective Date and Revisions
This manual supersedes all prior versions. Employees must re-acknowledge
receipt within 10 days of any revision being issued.


Section 2: Work Hours and Scheduling
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 Standard Shifts
Clinical shifts: 8-hour (days/evenings) or 12-hour (nights).
Administrative shifts: standard 9 AM – 5 PM, Monday to Friday.
Shift schedules are posted 2 weeks in advance via the Hospital scheduling portal.

2.2 Overtime
Overtime is defined as any hours worked beyond 40 hours in a single work week.
All overtime must be pre-approved by the department head in writing.
Unapproved overtime will not be compensated and may result in disciplinary action.

2.3 Shift Swaps
Shift swap requests must be submitted at least 48 hours before the shift.
Both employees must hold equivalent clinical credentials for the swap to be approved.
Final approval rests with the Charge Nurse or Department Supervisor.

2.4 Attendance
Three unexcused absences within a 90-day rolling period result in a written warning.
Five unexcused absences within the same period result in termination.
A no-call no-show counts as 2 unexcused absences.
Absences due to documented medical emergencies must be reported to HR within
3 business days with supporting documentation.


Section 3: Code of Conduct
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

3.1 Professional Behavior
All staff must treat patients, families, and colleagues with dignity and respect
at all times — in person, by phone, and in all written communications.

3.2 Dress Code
Clinical staff: hospital-issued scrubs and ID badge mandatory at all times.
Administrative staff: business casual attire; visible ID badge required.
Visible tattoos on the neck or face must be covered during patient-facing duties.
Open-toed shoes are prohibited in all clinical areas.

3.3 Social Media
Staff must NEVER post photographs, descriptions, or identifying details of patients
on any social media platform, even without using the patient's name.
Violations are grounds for immediate suspension pending investigation and possible
termination and regulatory referral.

3.4 Workplace Violence
Zero tolerance for any physical or verbal violence, threats, or intimidation.
Any incident must be reported to the Security Office and HR within 2 hours.
Security Office: ext. 5500 (internal) or +1 (503) 555-0150 (external).

3.5 Gifts
Staff may not accept gifts valued over $20 from patients, families, or vendors.
Gifts above this threshold must be declined or donated to the Hospital's patient
comfort fund.


Section 4: Patient Privacy and Confidentiality
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 Confidentiality Obligation
All patient health information (PHI) is strictly confidential. Staff may only
access patient records that are directly relevant to their assigned duties.

4.2 Prohibited Disclosures
PHI must never be shared with:
  - Family members without explicit written patient consent
  - Media or journalists under any circumstances
  - Other healthcare providers not involved in the patient's current care
  - Personal social media accounts or messaging apps

4.3 Breach Reporting
Any suspected or confirmed PHI breach must be reported to the Privacy Officer
within 1 hour of discovery.
Privacy Officer: Sarah Kim | privacy@riversidegh.org | +1 (503) 555-0101
Failure to report within 1 hour may result in personal regulatory liability.

4.4 Device Security
Patient data must never be stored on personal mobile devices, personal laptops,
or personal cloud storage. Hospital-issued encrypted devices only.
Lost or stolen hospital devices must be reported to IT Security within 30 minutes.
IT Security: helpdesk@riversidegh.org | +1 (503) 555-0175


Section 5: Medication and Clinical Safety
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

5.1 Medication Administration
Only licensed clinical staff may administer medications.
All medication orders must be verified against the patient chart before administration.
Double-verification is mandatory for high-alert medications (insulin, heparin,
chemotherapy agents, and potassium chloride).

5.2 Medication Errors
Any medication error or near-miss must be reported in the incident reporting system
within 4 hours of occurrence, regardless of patient outcome.
Intentional failure to report a medication error is grounds for immediate termination
and regulatory referral.

5.3 Controlled Substances
Controlled substances require dual-staff verification for withdrawal from the
medication dispensary. Any discrepancy must be reported to the Pharmacy Director
and the Charge Nurse immediately.

5.4 Personal Substance Use
Staff must not report to work under the influence of alcohol or any non-prescribed
substance. Random testing may be conducted for safety-sensitive roles.
Post-incident testing is mandatory following any adverse patient event.


Section 6: Workplace Safety
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

6.1 PPE Requirements
Gloves are mandatory for all patient contact. Surgical masks mandatory in all
patient-facing areas. N95 respirators required in airborne-precaution rooms.
PPE must be donned before entering and safely removed before leaving the zone.

6.2 Needle-Stick and Exposure
Any needle-stick or blood/body fluid exposure must be:
  1. Reported to the supervisor immediately.
  2. Assessed by Occupational Health within 2 hours.
  3. Documented in the incident system within 4 hours.
Post-exposure prophylaxis (PEP) must begin within 2 hours of blood-borne exposure.

6.3 Patient Lifting
Manual patient lifts over 35 lbs require a minimum of 2 staff members.
Mechanical lift equipment must be used for patients over 200 lbs whenever available.
Failure to use available lift equipment resulting in staff injury may affect
workers' compensation claims.

6.4 Emergency Codes
Code Blue (cardiac arrest): Call ext. 5555; begin CPR if trained.
Code Red (fire): Activate nearest pull station; use RACE protocol.
Code Silver (weapon/threat): Lockdown nearest room; do not confront.
All staff must complete emergency code training annually.


Section 7: Leave and Benefits
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

7.1 Paid Time Off (PTO)
Full-time employees accrue 1.25 PTO days per month (15 days per year).
Part-time employees accrue PTO on a pro-rated basis.
Maximum PTO balance: 30 days. Accrual stops above this cap.

7.2 Sick Leave
10 sick days per year, non-accruing. Must be used in the year earned.
Sick leave requires notification to the supervisor before the start of the shift
or within 1 hour of shift start if sudden illness.

7.3 Parental Leave
Primary caregiver: 12 weeks fully paid parental leave.
Secondary caregiver: 4 weeks fully paid parental leave.
Leave must begin within 6 months of birth, adoption, or foster placement.

7.4 FMLA
The Hospital complies with FMLA for eligible employees (12+ months of service,
1,250+ hours worked in the past 12 months). FMLA provides up to 12 weeks of
unpaid, job-protected leave per year.

7.5 Bereavement
3 paid days for immediate family (spouse, child, parent, sibling).
1 paid day for extended family (grandparent, in-law, aunt/uncle).


Section 8: Disciplinary Procedures
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

8.1 Progressive Discipline
Step 1: Verbal warning (documented in personnel file).
Step 2: Written warning.
Step 3: Final written warning with performance improvement plan (30 days).
Step 4: Termination.

8.2 Immediate Termination Offenses
The following result in immediate termination without progressive steps:
  - PHI breach or unauthorized patient record access
  - Patient abuse or neglect
  - Theft of hospital property or patient belongings
  - Reporting to work under the influence
  - Falsifying medical or incident records
  - Physical violence against a patient, visitor, or staff member

8.3 Appeal Process
Employees may appeal a disciplinary decision to the HR Director within 5 business
days of receiving written notice. The appeal panel decision is final.
